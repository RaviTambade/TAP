
## How AI is Changing Skills, Coding, and Careers

The discussion highlights an important shift happening in the technology world.

### 1️⃣ Coding vs Software Engineering

One important insight is the difference between **coding** and **engineering**.

* **Coding** → Writing syntax, functions, and small pieces of logic.
* **Software Engineering** → Designing systems, solving user problems, building architecture, integrating components.

AI models are already becoming very good at **coding tasks**.
But **software engineering thinking** still requires human understanding.

So the future engineer must focus more on:

* System design 🧠
* Understanding user needs 👥
* Architecture thinking 🏗️
* Product usefulness 🎯

The value will shift from **writing code** to **designing solutions**.

## 2️⃣ The Comparative Advantage Model

AI will not replace humans completely overnight.

Instead, work will look like this:

```
Human contribution → 5%
AI contribution → 95%
```

But that **5% becomes extremely valuable** because it guides the system.

Example roles in that 5%:

* Product thinking
* System architecture
* Problem framing
* Ethical judgment
* Decision making

A person managing AI tools can become **20x more productive**.

## 3️⃣ Industries With Strong Tailwinds

For someone starting a career today, good opportunities may exist in areas connected to AI.

Examples include:

### AI Infrastructure

* GPUs
* Compute infrastructure
* AI platforms

Companies like **NVIDIA** benefit because AI needs massive computing power.

### Semiconductor Engineering

Chip design and hardware engineering will grow strongly.

Companies like **TSMC**, **Intel**, and **AMD** are key players.

### AI Application Layer

Building applications on top of AI platforms.

Examples:

* AI copilots
* intelligent automation tools
* knowledge assistants
* decision systems

### Human-Centered Professions

Jobs that involve deep human interaction:

* healthcare
* education
* psychology
* coaching
* leadership

AI can assist these fields but cannot fully replace human empathy.

## 4️⃣ Physical World + Intelligence

Another strong future opportunity is combining **technology with the physical world**.

Examples:

* robotics
* healthcare technology
* manufacturing automation
* logistics intelligence

These require both **engineering knowledge and real-world understanding**.

## 5️⃣ The Most Important Skill: Critical Thinking

In the AI era, **information will become extremely easy to generate**.

AI can produce:

* articles
* videos
* images
* code
* fake information

Because of this, the most important skill may become:

**Critical Thinking**

The ability to ask:

* Is this information real?
* What is the evidence?
* What is the logic behind this claim?

People who can **separate truth from noise** will be very valuable.

## 6️⃣ The Risk of “Deskilling”

AI can make people more productive.

But if used poorly, it can also reduce skills.

Example:

If students use AI to **write assignments without thinking**, they lose learning ability.

But if AI is used as:

* a tutor
* a collaborator
* a code reviewer

then it **enhances learning instead of replacing it**.


## 7️⃣ Learning to Use AI is Like Learning an Instrument

Using AI effectively is a skill.

Just like playing the piano 🎹, it requires:

* practice
* experimentation
* learning how to ask good questions

This skill is often called **prompt engineering**, but more broadly it is:

**AI collaboration skill.**


# Final Mentor Insight

The future engineer should not ask:

**“How do I compete with AI?”**

Instead ask:

**“How do I work with AI to build better systems?”**

The engineers who will succeed are those who combine:

```
AI tools
+ Critical thinking
+ Human understanding
+ System design
```

Because in the AI era, the real role of humans may be:

**Defining the problem, guiding the intelligence, and ensuring the system serves people.**
